Our teaching method

How one English lesson actually works

Research on vocabulary retention is clear: a word needs to reappear many times, in different forms, before it sticks. So every lesson is built around a small set of words that travels through the whole unit.

1

Meet the words

5–7 new words, introduced with meaning, sound, and an example

2

Read & listen

The same words appear in a short text and audio clip

3

Fill in the blanks

Practice choosing the right word in context

4

Match & recall

Match words to meanings under light time pressure

5

Speak & write

Use the same words in your own sentence or short recording

↻
The point isn't the exercises — it's the repetition. The same 5–7 words follow the learner through reading, listening, writing and speaking in one unit, then resurface again in later review lessons. That's what makes them stick, not any single clever activity.
